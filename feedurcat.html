Protect yourself from cookies!

Your computer's browser is probably full of cookies, and companies use that information to advertise to you. This is just one aspect of protecting your privacy. Companies still use non-cookie alternatives to track you across websites and even clearing your cookies cannot stop that. By changing your settings you can protect yourself while browsing. There are first-party cookies and third-party cookies.

First-party cookies are what we have been talking about this whole time, what the site initially uses to track you.

1. Clear cookies regularly: You can delete cookies regularly browser settings periodically to reduce the amount of personal data stored on your device.

2. Use incognit or private browsing mode: Browsers have modes that prevent cookies from being stored after you close the browsing window. This can help to prevent long-term tracking.

3. Block third-party cookies: Mosst modern browsers offer settings to block third-party cookies. You can activate this feature in your browser's privacy settings.

4. Use cookie management tools: Some browser extensions and apps allow you to manage cookies in a moe detailed way, blocking certain cookies or only accepting them from trusted sites.

5. Enable "Do Not Track" (DNT) setting: While not always respected by websites, this setting lets websites know that you do not want to be tracked. You can enable it in your browser's privacy settings.

6. Use privacy-focused browsers: Some browsers such as Brave or Firefox are designed to block cookies and trackers by default. Additionally, they offer better privacy than more mainstream browsers.

7. Review privacy policies: When visiting websites, check their cookie policy to understand what data they are collecting and how they are using it. You can usually customize cookie preferences when prompted.

8. Use a VPN: A Virtual Private Network (VPN) can help mask your IP address and encrypt your internet traffic, making it harder for cookies to track your activity and location.

How You Can Protect Yourself in This Changing Online Environment:

→ Use Privacy-Focused Browsers: Switch to browsers like Brave or Mozilla Firefox as they will block third-party cookies and trackers by default. I personally use DuckDuckGo on my mobile devices.

→ Adopt Privacy Tools: Consider using a browser extension like uBlock origin (which I also use) to block tracking scripts and cookies.

→ Be Mindful of Permissions: Always read cookie consent banners and customize your prefernces to limit cross website tracking.

→ Stay Informed: Make sure to keep up with privacy regulations like GDPR and CCPA which will continue to evolve in response to the new developments around cookies and data mining.
